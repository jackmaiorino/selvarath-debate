The World of Selvarath
Geography
Selvarath consists of three regions separated by the Keth Range, a mountain chain running north-to-south through the continent's center. Passes through the Keth are navigable only between the 4th and 8th months of the Selvarath calendar (roughly spring through early autumn); winter closures average 19 weeks. West of the Keth lies the Verdane Basin, a broad lowland with two navigable rivers, the Osse and the Mirren, which converge 40 kilometers from the sea at the city of Confluence. The Verdane soil supports grain cultivation; average annual yield records indicate three harvests per decade fail partially or fully due to flooding. East of the Keth is the Taran Plateau, an elevated semi-arid zone where rainfall averages 310mm per year. The Taran supports pastoralism and limited dry-crop agriculture. A third region, the Shel Coast, runs along the southern shore and is accessible from both the Verdane Basin and the Taran Plateau by separate coastal roads that do not connect to each other except through the Keth passes or by sea. The Shel Coast holds the continent's only deep-water harbor at the port of Vethara.
Peoples and Polities
The Ossene inhabit the Verdane Basin. Their polity, the Ossene Compact, is governed by a council of 12 Wardens, each representing a river district. Wardenship passes to the eldest surviving child regardless of sex. Compact decisions require 9 of 12 Warden votes. The Ossene practice collective granary storage; by law, 20% of each harvest is deposited in district granaries administered by the Wardens.
The Taran Confederacy is a coalition of 7 clans on the Taran Plateau. Leadership rotates among clan heads on a 3-year cycle, with the current holder called the Vek. Inheritance within clans is matrilineal: property and clan membership pass through the mother's line. The Confederacy has no permanent capital; the Vek's home settlement serves as the administrative center during their term.
The Shel are a coastal trading people with no unified polity. Shel settlements are governed by merchant councils whose composition is determined by assessed wealth, reassessed every 6 years. Vethara is the largest Shel settlement and functions as a de facto regional hub, though it has no formal authority over other Shel towns. The Shel practice a contract-bond system: major commercial agreements are registered with a neutral arbiter called the Ledgerman, and disputes are resolved by binding arbitration rather than litigation.
Historical Events
The Keth Blockade (Year 0–4): The Taran Confederacy closed the two main Keth passes for 4 consecutive years following a dispute over toll rates. The Ossene Compact lost access to eastern tin, required for bronze production. This forced the Compact to develop iron-smelting, which it had previously imported from the Shel Coast. By Year 6, Compact iron output exceeded prior bronze output by volume. The Confederacy's toll revenue fell 60% during the Blockade due to diverted Shel sea-trade.
The Flood Compact (Year 31): Three consecutive flood-years in the Verdane Basin destroyed 70% of stored grain reserves. The Ossene negotiated emergency grain imports from Taran dry-crop surpluses. The resulting treaty, the Flood Compact, established a mutual reserve obligation: each party would maintain a 90-day grain reserve accessible to the other in declared emergencies. The Shel were not party to this agreement.
The Vethara Succession (Year 67–71): The Ledgerman of Vethara died without a registered successor. Two merchant factions contested the position for 4 years. During this period, 38% of registered contracts lapsed or were disputed. The Ossene Compact, which held significant trade contracts through Vethara, intervened diplomatically and brokered the Vethara Resolution, which created a 3-person Ledgerman panel rather than a single officer. The Compact received preferential arbitration fees in exchange.
The Eastern Drought (Year 89–96): Seven years of below-average rainfall reduced Taran pasture capacity. Two Confederacy clans, Veth and Sorath, moved herds permanently into contested lowland areas near the Keth foothills. The Compact disputed this as territorial encroachment. No formal resolution was reached; the clans remain in the foothills, now in Year 112.
The Iron Tariff (Year 104): The Compact imposed a 30% export tariff on iron goods sold through Vethara, citing infrastructure costs. The Shel Ledgerman panel ruled this violated the Vethara Resolution's most-favored-trade clause. The Compact disputes the panel's jurisdiction over tariff policy. The tariff remains in effect.
Present Situation (Year 112)
Three unresolved tensions currently structure Selvarath politics. First, Veth and Sorath clan herds occupy Keth foothill land the Compact considers its territory; no boundary treaty exists. Second, the Iron Tariff dispute is unresolved, with the Shel threatening to revoke Compact preferential arbitration fees. Third, the Flood Compact's mutual reserve obligation has not been tested, but Taran dry-crop yields have declined 18% over the past decade, and it is unclear whether current Taran reserves meet the 90-day threshold. Whether this decline triggers any Compact obligation or constitutes a breach is disputed between the parties.

The World of Vethun Sarak
Geography
Vethun Sarak occupies an inland basin ringed on the north and east by the Ashwall Mountains and open to the south and west across a broad steppe. The basin contains two permanent lakes, Kem and Orath, connected by the Kestal River. Kem is freshwater and supports agriculture along its northern shore; Orath is brackish and unsuitable for irrigation but yields significant salt harvests. The Kestal runs 210 kilometers between the lakes and drops 180 meters in elevation, making upstream navigation impossible but generating reliable water power at three points along its length. The steppe to the south and west has no significant natural barriers for approximately 600 kilometers; movement across it is limited in practice by water availability, with reliable sources spaced an average of 85 kilometers apart. A fourth geographic feature, the Redlands, is a zone of iron-rich laterite soil occupying the southeastern basin margin. The Redlands support neither agriculture nor grazing but contain surface iron ore deposits that have been mined continuously for at least 200 years.
Peoples and Polities
The Kemari inhabit the northern Kem shore and the Kestal River valley. Their polity, the Kemari Assembly, consists of 24 seats allocated by settlement population, recounted every 8 years. Decisions require simple majority except for declarations of war and territorial cession, which require 18 votes. The Assembly appoints an Executor for administrative functions; the Executor serves at Assembly pleasure with no fixed term. Kemari inheritance is bilateral: property is divided equally among all surviving children. Accumulation of large private landholdings is structurally limited by this practice; the Assembly holds significant communal land as a result.
The Sarak Dominion controls the Redlands and the southern basin margin. The Dominion is governed by a hereditary Archon; succession passes to the designated heir, who need not be a blood relative and is named publicly at least 5 years before the current Archon's death or abdication. If an Archon dies without a designated heir, a council of 7 District Heads selects a successor by unanimous vote; a failed vote triggers a 60-day deliberation period after which selection requires only 5 of 7. The Dominion has never failed to seat an Archon within 60 days. The Dominion's economy depends primarily on iron extraction and processing; it imports nearly all food.
The Orath Wanderers are a non-sedentary people who move across the southern steppe in 14 recognized bands. Bands are led by a Speaker elected by adult members for a term ending at the next major gathering, called the Massing, held every 4 years at the western shore of Lake Orath. The Massing elects an inter-band council of 14 Speakers and resolves disputes between bands. Wanderer social organization is age-graded: individuals move through four recognized life stages, each carrying different rights and obligations, with transitions determined by community assessment rather than fixed age. Salt harvesting rights on Lake Orath are allocated among bands at each Massing by a formula based on band population counts conducted during the gathering.
Historical Events
The Kestal Compact (Year 0): Following 30 years of intermittent conflict over Kestal River water rights, the Kemari Assembly and the Sarak Dominion signed the Kestal Compact, establishing that upstream Kemari irrigation draws may not reduce Kestal flow at the midpoint marker below 40% of the 10-year average. Measurement is conducted jointly twice yearly. The Compact has no enforcement mechanism beyond mutual agreement; neither party may appeal to an external authority under its terms.
The First Massing Dispute (Year 22): Two Wanderer bands, Thresh and Orath-Kem, submitted conflicting population counts at the Year 22 Massing, each claiming a larger count than independent estimates suggested was plausible. The inter-band council ruled that both counts be reduced by 15% for allocation purposes. Thresh accepted the ruling; Orath-Kem withdrew from the Massing and did not participate in salt allocation for 8 years. Orath-Kem rejoined at Year 30 Massing under terms that have not been publicly disclosed by the council.
The Iron Crisis (Years 47–53): A sustained drop in Redlands ore yield, attributed at the time to surface deposit exhaustion, reduced Dominion iron output by 40% over 6 years. The Dominion increased food import costs by drawing down reserves and negotiating 3 short-term grain loans from the Kemari Assembly, totaling 8,400 standard weight units, at an interest rate of 8% per 3-year period. New subsurface deposits were located in Year 53; output recovered to 90% of prior levels by Year 58. The loans were repaid in full by Year 61. During the crisis, the Dominion reduced its District Head council from 7 to 6 by merging two eastern districts; the merger has not been reversed.
The Dry Passage (Year 74): An extended drought reduced steppe water sources; 3 of the 14 reliable sources failed entirely for at least 2 consecutive years. Two Wanderer bands, Kural and Mesht, moved north into the Kem agricultural zone. The Kemari Assembly permitted temporary settlement under a 5-year residency agreement. Kural departed at the agreement's expiration; Mesht remained and was granted permanent residency by a 15-vote Assembly decision in Year 81. Mesht is now partially sedentary, with approximately 60% of members maintaining fixed residences. Their Speaker continues to attend the Massing, but Mesht's status as a Wanderer band for salt allocation purposes has been challenged by three other bands since Year 82.
The Watermill Dispute (Year 91–99): The Kemari Assembly licensed a private operator to construct a fourth Kestal watermill at kilometer 140. The Sarak Dominion argued this installation reduced downstream flow variability in ways that violated the Kestal Compact's spirit, though flow at the midpoint marker remained above the 40% threshold throughout. Arbitration was attempted twice; both attempts failed when the parties could not agree on an arbitrator. The mill has operated continuously since Year 93. The Dominion ceased participating in joint flow measurements from Year 95 to Year 101.
Present Situation (Year 112)
Four tensions remain active. First, the Kestal Compact's midpoint measurement has shown the flow approaching the 40% threshold in 3 of the last 6 measurements; the Kemari Assembly disputes the Dominion's measurement methodology, and the Dominion has proposed revising the threshold to 45%. Second, Mesht's salt allocation status is unresolved; the Year 112 Massing, scheduled for next year, will be required to rule on whether Mesht qualifies as a band in good standing. Third, the Sarak Dominion's current Archon, now in the 34th year of rule, has not yet designated a successor, creating succession uncertainty; the District Head council has 6 members rather than the 7 specified in the succession protocol following the Year 53 merger. Fourth, Orath-Kem band's undisclosed Year 30 Massing agreement has become relevant again: Orath-Kem claims it contains a provision granting them a fixed minimum salt allocation regardless of population count, a claim three other bands deny and the council has declined to confirm or deny publicly.

The World of Carath Norn
Geography
Carath Norn occupies a peninsula bounded on three sides by the Sullen Sea. The peninsula is divided longitudinally by the Spine, a ridge of low but steep hills running 340 kilometers from the northern interior to the southern coast. The Spine is not impassable but limits east-west wheeled transport to three natural gaps, called the Low Gates, located at kilometers 80, 190, and 310 from the north. The western slope of the Spine receives significantly more rainfall than the eastern; western annual averages run 480–520mm against eastern averages of 190–230mm. The western zone supports mixed farming; the eastern zone supports only dryland grain and extensive grazing. A third geographic zone, the Norn Delta, occupies the northern 80 kilometers of the peninsula where the Norn River fans into the Sullen Sea across six distributary channels. The Delta is fertile but flood-prone; settlement is restricted to raised ground covering roughly 30% of the Delta's total area. The Delta's channels are navigable by shallow-draft vessels year-round and by larger vessels only during the 5th through 9th months.
Peoples and Polities
The Caran occupy the western slope and the Delta. Their polity, the Caran Directorate, is administered by a Director appointed for a 10-year term by a college of 30 Factors, each representing a registered trade guild. The Director may be removed mid-term only by a 24-of-30 Factor vote. Inheritance of Factor seats passes within guild membership by internal election, not by bloodline. The Directorate maintains a professional standing army of approximately 4,000, funded by a transit tax levied at each Low Gate.
The Dresh occupy the eastern slope. They are organized into hereditary lordships; there are currently 11 active lordships of varying size. A lord's title passes to the eldest son, or in the absence of male heirs, to the eldest daughter's husband. Lordships with no qualifying heir revert to a collective body called the Dresh Moot, which has 11 seats filled by the remaining lords. The Moot elects a Paramount Lord from among its members when it controls 4 or more seats. It currently controls 2.
The Norn Compact is a loose association of Delta fishing and trading settlements with no hereditary leadership. Each settlement sends one delegate to a seasonal assembly, the Compact Meet, held at the start of months 3, 6, and 9. Decisions require simple majority of attending delegates. Attendance is not compulsory; quorum requires only 6 of the 14 registered settlements. The Compact has no standing military; it contracts armed escorts from the Caran Directorate under a renewable 7-year agreement, currently in its third renewal.
Historical Events
The Gate Wars (Years 0–14): Control of the three Low Gates was contested between early Caran and Dresh polities for 14 years. The conflict ended with the Treaty of Morven, which assigned gate administration to whichever party held the nearest western settlement, giving the Caran effective control of all three. The treaty also fixed transit tax rates and required equal access for Dresh merchants. The Dresh lord who negotiated the treaty, Arven of House Drath, was assassinated 2 years after signing; the circumstance of the killing was never formally adjudicated.
The Delta Compact Formation (Year 38): Following a navigational dispute in which Caran vessels blockaded two Delta channels for 11 weeks, the Delta settlements unified into the Norn Compact. The blockade had been triggered by a Caran claim that Delta pilots were underreporting cargo weights to reduce gate-transit fees. An independent count commissioned by the settlements found a 12% average underreporting rate across 6 sampled vessels, though the methodology was disputed by Compact delegates.
The Dry Decade (Years 55–64): Eastern slope rainfall fell below 150mm annually for 9 of 10 years. Three Dresh lordships collapsed economically; their lands reverted to the Moot. The Moot briefly held 5 seats and elected a Paramount Lord for the only time in recorded Carath Norn history. The Paramount Lord negotiated grain import rights from the Caran Directorate at fixed prices for 15 years. That agreement expired in Year 79 and was not renewed; grain now trades at market rates.
The Sixth Channel Incident (Year 83): A Caran military vessel pursuing a smuggling suspect entered the sixth Delta distributary without Compact notification, required under the escort agreement. The vessel grounded and was refloated with Compact assistance. The Directorate paid a formal penalty of 200 standard weight units of iron, as specified in the escort contract, but did not acknowledge the entry as a violation, characterizing it as an emergency pursuit. The Compact recorded it as a violation in its assembly minutes. The escort agreement was renewed the following year without amendment to the notification clause.
The Moot Consolidation (Year 98–101): House Drath, the largest eastern lordship, absorbed two neighboring lordships through marriage and a debt purchase over 3 years. House Drath now controls territory equivalent to roughly 35% of the eastern slope by area. The current lord, Sera Drath, is the first woman to hold the Drath title under a disputed inheritance in which two male cousins were passed over. The cousins' legal challenge was heard by the Moot and rejected by a 6–3 vote; the cousins have since left Carath Norn.
Present Situation (Year 112)
Three active tensions shape current politics. First, House Drath's accumulation of territory has prompted concern among remaining Dresh lords that Sera Drath may seek Paramount Lord status by acquiring two more lordships; the Moot currently has no mechanism to prevent consolidation. Second, the Norn Compact's escort agreement with the Directorate expires in Year 114 and renegotiation is underway; the Compact is seeking an amendment requiring prior notification in all circumstances, which the Directorate has not accepted. Third, a new Caran Factor election in Guild Twelve, the ironworkers' guild, is disputed: two candidates each claim a majority of member votes, and the sitting Director has declined to adjudicate, citing lack of jurisdiction over internal guild affairs. Guild Twelve's Factor seat has been vacant for 14 months, leaving the college at 29 members and the removal threshold effectively requiring 24-of-29.
